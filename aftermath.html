Where do the Leafs go from here, now that they've experienced the lowest of lows?

February 22, 2020 is the most humiliating regular-season loss in NHL history, according to both Leafs fans and analysts. One would think that Kyle Dubas, the Leafs' General Manager, would sell the team and throw everything he had built up in the gutter. The players said it themselves; it's a matter of 'willpower.' Even though the Leafs, according to Adam Wylde and Steve Dangle in the Steve Dangle Podcast, objectively have the best coaching / health / analyzing staff in the whole NHL.

Despite this, Toronto has turned over a new leaf. They won 3 straight games, including against the Tampa Bay Lightning, the best regular-season team so far. Many people are saying that the loss "humbled" the Leafs and reminded them of their drive to make the playoffs and win the Stanley Cup.

What about the Hurricanes?

The Hurricanes lose four straight after that game. As of the making of this website, they are one behind the Islanders for a wild card spot. They have the manpower to squeeze in at the final day (like they did last season), but no one knows.

And what about David Ayres, the star of the show?

David Ayres received the star treatment. From a media tour that took him to places like New York and Raleigh, to being the one to sound the sirens at the Hurricanes' next home game, to having his jersey sold in large numbers, Ayres was living the dream. He even has his stick in the Hockey Hall of Fame! On top of that, he has his own hockey card on Upper Deck. The card reads: "David Ayres, a 42-year-old maintenance operations manager and part-time Zamboni driver, was called into action as the emergency goaltender about halfway through the Carolina Hurricanes’ game against Toronto after both Carolina goaltenders were injured. In his surprise NHL debut, he helped Carolina to a 6-3 win over the Maple Leafs."
